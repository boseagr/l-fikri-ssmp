# Weather in Asia

Asia is currently experiencing a wide range of weather conditions across its vast and diverse landscape. East Asian countries such as Japan, China, and South Korea are in the midst of their summer monsoon season, with heavy rainfall and high humidity affecting many coastal and inland areas. Southeast Asia, including Thailand, Vietnam, and the Philippines, continues to see tropical conditions with frequent afternoon thunderstorms and warm temperatures throughout the region. The Indian subcontinent is deep into the southwest monsoon, bringing much-needed rainfall to India and Bangladesh after a hot and dry spring. Central Asian nations like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan are enjoying hot, dry summer weather with clear skies and minimal precipitation. Meanwhile, the Middle East remains under intense heat, with temperatures in the Arabian Peninsula and Iraq regularly exceeding seasonal norms. Overall, Asia's enormous geographic extent means weather varies dramatically from region to region, but the dominant theme this time of year is the influence of the Asian monsoon system across the southern and eastern parts of the continent.
